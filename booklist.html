My Booklist

I love to read. With everything that happens in our busy lives, it is a rare pleasure to be able to sit down and explore a "world" other than our own for a few hours. These are just a few of the worlds I have explored recently, listed in reverse-chronological order.

Three Body Problem (三体)

By Liu Cixin (刘慈欣), translated by Ken Liu

I thought Three Body Problem to be a remarkable book, in the respect that it is perfectly able to incorporate elements of history, politics, and science fiction while allowing foreigners to gaze through a unique Chinese perspective on a number of issues. The world-building and attention to detail is superb, and each plot line is neatly woven into a cohesive story. The premise of the novel — first contact — is unique in its portrayal as the author makes the salient point that extraterrestrial communication would simply evolve into another resource to monopolize. In other words, humanity would not be united by an alien threat; instead, we would seek to leverage it in any way to destroy ourselves. Indeed, we see this today with COVID-19. Instead of a unified, worldwide response, we have again returned to pointing fingers, misinformation, and incompetance, but to what end? The threat still remains. Nonetheless, Three Body Problem is a masterwork of science fiction and seeks to illuminate some of the darker aspects of our nature. I recommend this novel to anyone remotely interested in science fiction and space.
